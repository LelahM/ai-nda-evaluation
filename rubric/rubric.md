# NDA Evaluation Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **0**: Missing - The category or required element is absent from the draft
- **1**: Present but weak, vague, or creates legal risk - Element exists but is incomplete, ambiguous, or insufficient for enforceability
- **2**: Clear and acceptable for attorney review - Element is present, specific, and follows standard legal practice

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Parties and Purpose
**What earns 0**: No party identification or missing legal entity form  
**What earns 1**: Parties named but missing jurisdiction, legal form, or business purpose  
**What earns 2**: Both parties identified with legal form (Corp, LLC, etc.), jurisdiction, and business purpose stated

- Identifies both parties with legal form (corporation, LLC, etc.)
- States business purpose for information exchange
- Includes effective date or execution date placeholder

**Notes field**: Record observations with direct quotes from draft

### 2. Confidential Information Definition
**What earns 0**: No definition provided or only circular language ("confidential means confidential")  
**What earns 1**: Definition exists but is overly broad ("all information") or too vague to enforce  
**What earns 2**: Specific definition with examples, avoids overbroad language, enforceable scope

- Defines what information is protected
- Scope is specific enough to be enforceable
- Avoids overly broad or circular definitions

**Notes field**: Quote the definition, note any vagueness or overreach

### 3. Exclusions
**What earns 0**: No exclusions clause present  
**What earns 1**: Partial exclusions (e.g., only public information, missing independent development or required disclosure)  
**What earns 2**: Standard exclusions present (public, prior knowledge, independent development, lawful third-party, required legal disclosure)

- Lists standard exceptions (public information, prior knowledge, independent development, lawful third-party disclosure, required legal disclosure)
- Absence of exclusions creates enforceability risk

**Notes field**: List which exceptions are present or missing

### 4. Obligations (Use and Care)
**What earns 0**: No use restrictions or care standards specified  
**What earns 1**: Vague obligations ("keep confidential") without specific use limitations or care standard  
**What earns 2**: Specific use limitations tied to business purpose, defined care standard (reasonable care or same-as-own)

- Limits use of confidential information to stated purpose
- Specifies care standard (e.g., reasonable care, same care as own information)
- Addresses who may access the information

**Notes field**: Quote key obligation language

### 5. Permitted Disclosures
**What earns 0**: No guidance on who may access confidential information  
**What earns 1**: Mentions employees/agents but lacks conditions or compelled disclosure procedure  
**What earns 2**: Specifies permitted recipients, conditions for disclosure, notice requirements for compelled disclosure

- Addresses disclosure to employees, agents, or advisors
- Specifies conditions for such disclosure
- Addresses notice requirements for compelled disclosure

**Notes field**: Note any gaps in disclosure controls

### 6. Term and Survival
**What earns 0**: No term specified or missing survival provisions  
**What earns 1**: Term stated but survival unclear or absent  
**What earns 2**: Clear agreement duration and explicit survival of confidentiality obligations post-termination

- Specifies how long the agreement is effective
- Addresses survival of confidentiality obligations after termination
- Absence creates uncertainty about obligation duration

**Notes field**: Quote term language, note if survival is missing

### 7. Return or Destruction of Materials
**What earns 0**: No provision for return or destruction  
**What earns 1**: Mentions return/destruction but lacks trigger or procedure  
**What earns 2**: Clear return/destruction requirement with specified trigger (termination, request) and procedure

- Requires return or destruction of confidential materials
- Specifies trigger (end of relationship, upon request)
- Absence leaves no mechanism for information control

**Notes field**: Quote provision or note absence

### 8. Remedies
**What earns 0**: No remedies specified or only generic "legal action"  
**What earns 1**: Mentions damages but omits injunctive relief or equitable remedies  
**What earns 2**: Includes both monetary damages and injunctive relief, acknowledges irreparable harm

- Addresses available remedies for breach
- Includes injunctive relief for breach of confidentiality obligations
- Vague "legal action" language is insufficient

**Notes field**: Quote remedy language, note if injunctive relief is missing

### 9. Governing Law and Venue
**What earns 0**: No governing law or jurisdiction clause  
**What earns 1**: Governing law stated but jurisdiction/venue missing or unclear  
**What earns 2**: Both governing law and jurisdiction/dispute resolution clearly specified

- Specifies which state or country's law applies
- Addresses jurisdiction or dispute resolution
- Absence creates enforceability uncertainty

**Notes field**: Quote governing law clause or note absence

### 10. No License / No Partnership / Assignment
**What earns 0**: No clarification on IP rights, entire agreement, or assignment  
**What earns 1**: Partial coverage (e.g., entire agreement only, missing IP license clarification)  
**What earns 2**: Clarifies no IP license, addresses entire agreement/amendments/severability, assignment restrictions if relevant

- Clarifies that no IP license is granted
- Addresses entire agreement, amendments, severability
- Addresses assignment restrictions if relevant

**Notes field**: Note which provisions are present

### 11. Clarity and Consistency
**What earns 0**: Undefined terms, inconsistent references, ambiguous pronouns throughout  
**What earns 1**: Some defined terms but inconsistent usage or unclear references remain  
**What earns 2**: Defined terms used consistently, clear structure, unambiguous obligations

- Uses defined terms consistently
- Avoids ambiguous pronouns or references
- Structure supports understanding of obligations

**Notes field**: Identify any unclear or inconsistent language

## High-Risk Clause Gaps

If any of the following score **0**, flag the draft as high risk:
- Exclusions (Category 3)
- Term and Survival (Category 6)
- Return or Destruction (Category 7)
- Governing Law and Venue (Category 9)
