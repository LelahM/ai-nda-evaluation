# NDA Evaluation Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **0**: Inadequate or missing
- **1**: Present but incomplete or unclear  
- **2**: Comprehensive and well-structured

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Completeness (Essential Clauses)
Presence of core NDA provisions:
- Party identification and effective date
- Definition of confidential information
- Use restrictions and permitted purposes
- Obligations of receiving party
- Standard exceptions to confidentiality
- Return/destruction provisions
- Term and termination
- Remedies and enforcement mechanisms

### 2. Legal Structure
Professional legal document formatting:
- Clear section organization with headings
- Proper legal language and terminology
- Consistent clause numbering and structure
- Professional document formatting

### 3. Clarity and Precision
Understandability and specificity:
- Plain language where appropriate
- Defined terms for technical/business concepts
- Specific timeframes and deadlines
- Clear obligations and restrictions
- Avoidance of ambiguous language

### 4. Risk Mitigation
Identification and address of key legal risks:
- Broad but reasonable confidentiality scope
- Injunctive relief provisions
- Proper governing law and jurisdiction clauses
- Severability provisions
- Mutual vs. unilateral considerations where appropriate

### 5. Business Context Alignment
Appropriateness for stated business scenario:
- Relevant to specific business relationship
- Consideration of industry-specific needs
- Practical enforceability
- Balanced protection without overreach
